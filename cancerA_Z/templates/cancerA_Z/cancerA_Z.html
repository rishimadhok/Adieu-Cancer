Cancer A-Z

Brain Cancer

A brain tumor or intracranial neoplasm occurs when abnormal cells form within the brain. Symptoms may include headaches, seizures, problem with vision, vomiting, and mental changes. More specific problems may include difficulty in walking, speaking, and with sensation.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is cancer that develops from breast tissue. Signs of breast cancer may include a lump in the breast, a change in breast shape, dimpling of the skin, fluid coming from the nipple, or a red scaly patch of skin.

Leukemia

Leukemia, also spelled leukaemia, is a group of cancers that usually begin in the bone marrow and result in high numbers of abnormal white blood cells. Symptoms may include bleeding and bruising problems, feeling tired, fever, and an increased risk of infections.

Click to know more

Liver Cancer

Liver cancer, also known as hepatic cancer is a cancer that starts in the liver. Symptoms of liver cancer may include a lump or pain in the right side below the rib cage, swelling of the abdomen, yellowish skin, easy bruising, weight loss, and weakness.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer, also known as lung carcinoma, is a malignant lung tumor characterized by uncontrolled cell growth in tissues of the lung. Common symptoms are coughing, weight loss, shortness of breath, and chest pains.

Ovarian Cancer

Ovarian cancer is a cancer that forms in an ovary. When this process begins, there may be no or only vague symptoms. They become more noticeable as the cancer progresses. These may include bloating, pelvic pain, abdominal swelling, and loss of appetite, among others.

Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer, also known as carcinoma of the prostate, is the development of cancer in the prostate, a gland in the male reproductive system. It can lead to difficulty in urinating, blood in the urine, or pain in the pelvis, back or when urinating.

Click to know more

Skin Cancer

Skin cancers are cancers that arise from the skin. They are due to the development of abnormal cells that have the ability to spread to other parts of the body. Signs include a mole that has changed in size, shape, color, has irregular edges, has more than one color, is itchy or bleeds.

Thyroid Cancer

Thyroid cancer is a cancer originating from follicular or parafollicular thyroid cells. First symptom of thyroid cancer is a nodule in the thyroid region of the neck. Later symptoms include pain in the anterior region of the neck, changes in voice due to involvement of the laryngeal nerve.
